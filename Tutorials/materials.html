3D Printing Materials Overview

PLA (Polylactic Acid)

  • Rigid with decent strength
  • Low melting point, generally not suitable for outdoor use
  • Good for prototypes, enclosures, or lightweight mounts
  • Minimal warping, adheres well to most build surfaces

PETG (Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol)

  • Stronger and less brittle than PLA
  • Good for functional parts that need durability
  • Resistant to moisture and some chemicals
  • Can be stringy if printed too fast and needs slightly higher temperatures

PVA (Polyvinyl Alcohol)

  • Water-soluble support material for the dual-extruder printer (Flashforge Creator II Pro)
  • Used when printing complex overhangs that need temporary support
  • Use only when conventional supports would not be an option.
  • Store in a dry place to prevent it from absorbing moisture

TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethane)

  • Flexible, rubber-like material
  • Good for wearable parts, live hinges, or items that need bending/stretching
  • Requires slower printing and fine-tuned settings
